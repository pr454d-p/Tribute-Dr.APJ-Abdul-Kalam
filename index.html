Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

The man who taught us to dream

Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

  • 1931 - Born on Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 - Graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappali
  • 1955 - Enrolled at the MIT to study aerospace engineering
  • 1960 - Joined DRDO as Chief Scientist
  • 1965 - Started work rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1969 - Transferred to the ISRO
  • 1980 - Resposiable for the development of AGNI and PRITHVI Missiles
  • 1981 - Padma Bhushan Government of India
  • 1990 - Padma Vibhushan Government of India
  • 1992 - Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the PM and the Secreatary of DRDO
  • 1997 - Bharat Ratna Government of India
  • 2002 - Served as the 11thPresident of India
  • 2012 - Launched mission for the youth of the nation called "What Can I Give Movement"
  • 2015 - Passes away on 27 July in Shilong Meghalaya

Kalam Sahab was a Rashtra Ratna first and then a Rashtrapati
